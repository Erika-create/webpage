    The Stages of Drug Discovery

    1. Discovery and Development

    Discovery

    4 General ways of Discovery:

    Countless compounds have a potential to be developed as a medical treatment. However, only a few of compounds are recognised as useful after preliminary testing for more research.

    • New perception to the procedure of disease.
    • Postive effects could be discovered through multiple experiments of molecular compounds.
    • Unexpected result from the current treatments.
    • Controlling genetic material or novel ways to treat the specific part of the body with the medical treatment thorough innovative technologies.

    Development

    Researchers tests the compounds for the certain informations after identifying the compounds. They test them on:

    • Details of absorption, distribution, metabolism, and excretion.
    • Procedure of action and its probable advantages.
    • The optimum dosage.
    • Most favourable method of dosage.
    • Toxicity caused by any side effects or adverse events.
    • The alternative impact on various groups of human, for example gender, race, age, or ethnicity.
    • The way they interact with other medical treatments.

    2. Preclinical Research

    Toxicity must be tested in advance of utilised in human. 2 types of preclinical research are:

    • In vitro: Tests conducted on samples such as blood or tissue which is taken out of the human body is In vitro diagnostics (IVD).
    • In vivo: Refers to the test conducted on living organisms, such as animals.
    Typically, preclinical research is not conducted largely. Nevertheless, it has a crucial role in providing information about the dosing and toxicity, to determine if the drug should be attempted on humans.

    3. Clinical Research

    Clinical research is a study or tests that is conducted on humans. For each of the Clinical Research Phases, scientists acknowledge the objective and start the Investigational New Drug Process (IND) while they arrange the clinical research.

    Designing Clinical Research

    A protocal is the plan designed which the clinical research follows. They include the information about:

    • Entry qualification of the trial
    • The number of people in the sample
    • Period of the study
    • Needs of control group and alternative ways to lessen the bias
    • Dosage
    • Objective data of the trial
    • The method of data reveiwed and analysed

    IND

    Investigational New Drug (IND) application must be submitted by the drug researchers before the clinical trials, which needs to include:

    • In vivo study data with the animals and toxicity data
    • Construction information
    • Protocals
    • Any data from the human research conducted before
    • Information about the researcher

    Phase of Clinical Research

    Phase 1:
    Usually 20-100 healthy participants or the participants with the certain disease.
    Period of time: Couple of months.
    Purpose: To assure the safety and to get the information about the dosage.
    To the second phase, around 70% of drugs.

    Phase 2:
    Usually around few hundered participants with the certain disease.
    Period of time: Couple of months to 2 years.
    Purpose: Potency and side effects.
    To the third phase, around 33% of drugs.

    Phase 3:
    Usually 300-3000 participants with the certain disease.
    Period of time: 1-4 years.
    Purpose: Potency and checking of side effects.
    To the last phase, around 25-30%.

    Phase 4:
    Usually couple thousands participants with the certain disease.
    Purpose: Assuring the safety and checking the potency.

    Review and Approval

    The IND is reviewed by the FDA IND Review team within the 30 days, to check if there is any faults that could harm the people due to the clinical trial.
    The clinical trial could be:

    • Approved
    • Delayed or ordered to cutoff the the research from the clinical hold

    4. FDA Drug Review

    Once the drug researcher has sufficient amount of evidence to confirm that the medicine is safe and is useful, they can apply to sell in the market. For this, the team of FDA review would inspect the data of the medicine carefully if it should be allowed or not.

    New Drug Application

    The file of drug application is called New Drug Application (NDA), which consists of all the data of the drug, such as:

    • Safety information
    • Side effects from the drug
    • All of the data of the trials or studies
    • Method of usage

    FDA Review

    NDA would be determined if it is completed enough to be filed or not by FDA. When completed, NDA would be then examined by FDA, usually taking 6-10 months to be decided if it should be accpeted ot not. The decision is made carefully through:

    • Full examination by each person in the team of their section would be done.
    • To the clinical trial sites, the FDA inspectors go for supervision. They check if there is any falsehood, manipulation, or deterring of data.
    • All of the reviewed data would be assembled into "action package" by the project manager. The senior team of FDA official makes a decision.

    FDA Approval

    Once FDA decides that the medicine is safe and useful, "labeling" is done, which is to clarify and improve the prescribing information.
    In most cases, FDA questions or requires another trials and studies to eliminate any problems.
